# [Towards scalable robotic intervention of children with Autism Spectrum   Disorder using LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.00260)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Social robots have shown promise for interventions for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), but most existing solutions have limited scalability due to relying on finite, pre-defined responses the robot can give. 
- There is a need for more autonomous robotic systems that can generate personalized content and interactions with children to aid perspectives taking and understanding social situations.

Proposed Solution:
- The authors propose using a social robot (NAO) with a large language model (LLM) pipeline to generate content to teach perspective-taking skills.  
- The robot takes on roles of a stimulator (generates social situations and questions), prompter (presents multiple choice options) and reinforcer (praises correct answers).
- Two LLM pipelines are compared: GPT-2 + BART and GPT-2 + GPT-2. GPT-2 generates social situations, while BART or second GPT-2 generates corresponding questions and multiple choice options.
- Models are fine-tuned on the SOCIALIQA dataset containing scenarios for social reasoning.

Main Contributions:
- First work to use LLMs to automatically generate content for robotic intervention of children with ASD to teach perspective taking.
- Comparison of BART (sequence-to-sequence) versus GPT-2 (decoder-only) for text generation tasks using BERTscore and human evaluations.
- BART pipeline found to have better BERTscore and human evaluation scores. 
- Social situations generated by model evaluated by experts as appropriate for children with ASD.
- Proposed system acts as step towards more scalable and sustainable robotic interventions for ASD.


## Summarize the paper in one sentence.

 This paper proposes using a pipeline of GPT-2 and BART language models to automatically generate social situations, questions, and multiple choice options that a NAO robot can vocalize to teach perspective-taking skills to children with autism spectrum disorder.


## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 According to the paper, the main contributions are:

1. To the best of their knowledge, this is the first work that generates content to teach perspective-taking to children with ASD using large language models (LLMs).

2. They compare a sequence-to-sequence model (BART) with a decoder-based model (GPT-2) for text-generation tasks using BERTscore and also verify the outcome using human evaluations. 

3. They evaluate the text generation for appropriateness for children with ASD through human evaluators from the clinical and special education domains.

So in summary, the main contributions are using LLMs to automatically generate content to teach perspective-taking to children with ASD, comparing two LLM architectures (BART and GPT-2) for this text generation task, and evaluating the generated text for appropriateness for the target user group.
