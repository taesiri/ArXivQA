## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 This paper makes several key contributions:

1. It introduces a novel type of highly stretchable hydrogel, termed a "span network" hydrogel, which can stretch up to 260 times its original length. The key to achieving this high stretchability is the strategic incorporation of linear polyethylene oxide (PEO) chains between crosslinked polymer networks. 

2. It uses an artificial intelligence-based reaction predictor to propose two potential molecular mechanisms by which the linear PEO chains become incorporated into the crosslinked hydrogel networks. One mechanism involves radical-mediated attachment of PEO to the networks, while the other involves PEO chain scission and subsequent ester formation.

3. It provides experimental FTIR evidence supporting the proposed molecular mechanisms, including confirmation of ester formation between PEO hydroxyl end groups and carboxyl groups in the hydrogel networks. 

4. It demonstrates exceptional mechanical properties of the span network hydrogels, including the ability to stretch over 2000% of the original length. Stress relaxation experiments reveal unique behaviors not seen in typical hydrogels.

In summary, the key innovation is the development of a new class of highly stretchable hydrogels enabled by the strategic incorporation of linear polymer chains, along with the use of AI and experiments to elucidate the molecular mechanisms underlying the unique mechanical properties.
