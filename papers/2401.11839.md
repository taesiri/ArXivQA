 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem: There has been increasing research exploring the combination of AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), with social science. Contributions:

1) Provides a fresh categorization distinguishing and outlining the connections between using AI to study human society versus studying AI societies based on social science theories.

5) Discusses unique social behaviors exhibited among LLM agents from psychological, sociological, economic, political and linguistic perspectives that warrant further investigation.

In conclusion, as AI advances, studying its combination with social science from these dual lenses will become increasingly crucial. While AI cannot replace social scientists, it has potential for tight integration into the research process.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 This paper makes three main contributions:

1. It presents a perspective of revisiting AI and social science combinations from two directions: "AI for social science" which summarizes the application of AI at every stage of social science research, and "social science of AI" which systematically describes the intelligence level and characteristics of AI agents from a social science perspective. 

2. Based on the substantial progress facilitated by recent advancements in large language models to these two directions, it conducts a literature review, summarizing the research landscape, discussing the limitations of the existing research, and shedding light on potential future directions in the combination of AI and social science.

3. It collects and compares existing open source large language model-based simulation tools. These platforms can serve an effective foundation to facilitate the future researches of the above-mentioned two directions by allowing researchers to simulate human behavior and AI agent communities.

In summary, this paper provides a fresh perspective and comprehensive overview of the intersection between AI, specifically large language models, and social science by outlining two distinct directions, reviewing the state-of-the-art progress, and introducing simulation platforms to enable future research.
