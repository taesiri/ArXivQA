 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Persuasion is a crucial ability in human communication, but developing intelligent and anthropomorphic persuasive dialogue agents is challenging. 
- Persuasion involves complex psychological dynamics and requires incorporating cognitive strategies from cognitive psychology. Existing works lack a systematic framework and investigation from the cognitive psychology perspective.

Proposed Solution:
- The paper proposes the concept of Cognitive Strategy-enhanced Persuasive Dialogue Agent (CogAgent) which integrates cognitive strategies into persuasive dialogue modeling. 
- Three key cognitive strategies are formalized - persuasion strategy, topic path planning strategy, and argument structure prediction strategy. These aim to influence users' perceptions and guide coherent persuasive dialogues.
- A concept model and system architecture are presented to provide foundations for CogAgent research.

Main Contributions:
- Comprehensively investigates cognitive psychology theories for persuasive dialogues to inspire CogAgent designs.
- Formally defines persuasive cognitive strategies to establish CogAgent methodology.  
- Summarizes key techniques of existing CogAgent works categorized by cognitive strategies adopted, covering strategy incorporation mechanisms, planning algorithms, argument mining and more.
- Reviews available datasets and evaluation metrics, discusses open issues, and provides insights into future trends like model adaptivity, multi-party dialogues, and multimodality for CogAgent.

In summary, the paper presents a systematic framework and thorough investigation of cognitive strategy-enhanced persuasive dialogue agents, combining cognitive psychology and language generation to push forward this emerging field. The formalized concept model and research summary offer valuable guidance for future CogAgent development.
