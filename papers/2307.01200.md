# [Real-time Monocular Full-body Capture in World Space via Sequential   Proxy-to-Motion Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.01200)

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

Based on my reading, the central research question/hypothesis of this paper is: How can we develop a real-time monocular full-body motion capture system that produces accurate human motions with plausible foot-ground contact in world space?The key challenges the authors aim to address are:- Existing datasets lack diversity and accuracy in global motions due to simplified camera assumptions and depth ambiguity. This limits the accuracy of learning-based approaches.- It remains difficult for existing solutions to achieve real-time full-body capture while also being accurate in world space and ensuring physical plausibility.To tackle these issues, the main contributions of this paper are:- A sequential proxy-to-motion learning scheme using 2D skeleton sequences as proxy representations synthesized from accurate 3D motions. This provides more accurate supervision and alleviates generalization issues.- A contact-aware neural motion descent module that is aware of foot-ground contact and motion misalignment to refine predictions.- Sharing body-hand context information for better wrist pose compatibility in the full-body model.- A demonstration of real-time monocular full-body capture with plausible foot-ground contact in world space.In summary, the central hypothesis is that by using accurate proxy training data and contact-aware refinement, their method can achieve real-time, full-body motion capture with physical plausibility in world space, overcoming limitations of prior work. The experiments aim to validate this hypothesis.


## What is the main contribution of this paper?

Based on my reading of the paper, the main contributions appear to be:1. A new data generation strategy that synthesizes proxy-to-motion pairs from existing large-scale motion datasets and virtual camera settings. They use 2D skeleton sequences as proxy representations and associate them with accurate 3D rotational motion sequences in world space. This is aimed at overcoming accuracy and generalization issues with previous datasets.2. A contact-aware neural motion descent module that takes into account foot-ground contact status and motion misalignment with proxy observations. This is designed to produce more accurate and physically plausible motion predictions. 3. Leveraging body-hand context information in the network architecture to enable better compatibility between wrist poses and the full body model, for improved full-body motion capture.4. Demonstrating a real-time monocular full-body capture system with plausible foot-ground contact in world space. Their proposed learning-based solution can be trained end-to-end and run in real-time.In summary, the key contributions seem to be in data generation, network architecture design, and producing an effective real-time full-body motion capture system with physical plausibility in world space. The proxy data representation and contact-aware refinement modules appear central to enabling these contributions.

## How does this paper compare to other research in the same field?

Here is a summary of how I see this paper relating to other research on monocular full-body motion capture:- The paper tackles a very challenging problem - real-time, full-body motion capture from a single RGB camera. Other recent work has focused more on accuracy and robustness over speed.- For full-body modeling, the approach shares similarities to other works in using separate body/hand/face models integrated together. But the contributions seem to push the state-of-the-art forward in this challenging space.In summary, the paper makes several noteworthy contributions on top of related works, achieving compelling results on a very difficult problem.
