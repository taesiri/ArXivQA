# [Determinants of LLM-assisted Decision-Making](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.17385)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key aspects of the paper:

Problem: 
Large language models (LLMs) provide multifaceted assistance in human decision-making processes. However, research on the factors influencing LLM-assisted decision-making, referred to as "determinants", and their interactions is limited. Understanding these determinants can empower users and organizations to enhance decision quality when using LLMs.

Proposed Solution:
This paper conducts a comprehensive literature review across disciplines to identify technological, psychological and decision-specific determinants of LLM-assisted decision-making. For each determinant, the paper analyzes its impact on decision-making with LLMs and interactions with other factors, accompanied by scenario-based illustrations. 

Based on the literature analysis, the paper develops a "dependency framework" that visually systematizes the reciprocal interdependencies between the various determinants. This framework showcases that factors like trust, mental models of users, and information processing emerge as key aspects influencing LLM-assisted decision-making owing to their diverse interactions.  

Main Contributions:

- Structural overview of technological, psychological and decision-specific determinants impacting LLM-assisted decision-making based on a cross-disciplinary literature analysis 

- Dependency framework systematizing the interactions between determinants in terms of reciprocal interdependencies

- Detailed analysis of each determinant regarding its impact on LLM-assisted decision-making and potential interactions, including scenario-based demonstrations

- Identification of trust, mental models and information processing as pivotal factors in LLM-assisted decision-making

- Discussion of implications for users and organizations to optimize decision-making with LLMs and mitigate risks 

- Foundation for future empirical research on determinants and guidelines for training programs on LLM-assisted decision-making

In summary, this paper significantly advances the understanding of factors influencing human decision-making behavior with LLM support through its structural overview, dependency framework and comprehensive analysis. This knowledge can empower users and organizations to enhance decision quality when leveraging LLMs.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contributions of this paper are:

1. It provides a structural overview and detailed analysis of the technological, psychological, and decision-specific determinants that influence decision-making processes assisted by Large Language Models (LLMs). 

2. It develops a dependency framework that systematizes the potential interactions and interdependencies between the identified determinants of LLM-assisted decision-making. This framework visualize the reciprocal relationships through a feature diagram and dependency matrix.

3. It demonstrates the utility of the framework and analysis by illustrating the application of the findings across multiple real-world scenarios involving decisions made with LLM support. 

4. It discusses implications for improving decision quality in human-LLM collaboration, empowering both users and organizations. For instance, by enhancing awareness of determinants like over-reliance on LLMs.

5. The paper provides a foundation for future empirical investigations on factors influencing LLM-assisted decision-making, including experimental research on the impact of emotions or mental models.

In summary, the key contribution is the provision of a comprehensive, structured analysis of determinants in LLM-assisted decision-making and the development of an interaction framework that can inform strategies for improving decisions involving LLMs.
