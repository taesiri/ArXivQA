# [Hierarchical Contrastive Learning for Pattern-Generalizable Image   Corruption Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.14061)

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

Based on the abstract, this paper aims to develop a novel method for automatic corruption detection to enable blind image restoration without known corruption masks. The key ideas and contributions appear to be:- Proposing a hierarchical contrastive learning framework to detect corrupted regions by capturing intrinsic semantic distinctions between corrupted and uncorrupted regions. - Detecting corruption masks in a coarse-to-fine manner, first predicting a coarse mask with low-resolution contrastive learning, then refining uncertain areas with high-resolution contrastive learning.- Designing a hierarchical interaction mechanism to facilitate knowledge propagation of contrastive learning across scales and boost performance. - Developing a general blind image restoration model by integrating the hierarchical contrastive learning module, which can perform high-quality restoration without known masks.- Demonstrating superior performance over other methods on corruption detection and image restoration tasks like blind inpainting and watermark removal.- Showing strong generalization ability across different corruption patterns, without needing adaptation.In summary, the central hypothesis is that hierarchical contrastive learning to capture intrinsic semantic distinctions can enable precise corruption detection and in turn blind image restoration, with strong generalization across corruption patterns. The key innovation seems to be the hierarchical framework and interaction mechanism for contrastive learning.

## How does this paper compare to other research in the same field?

This paper introduces a novel method for automatic image corruption detection to enable blind image restoration without known corruption masks. Here are some key comparisons to other related work:- Most prior work on blind image inpainting assumes simple corruption patterns like constant noise, limiting real-world applicability. This paper tackles more complex and diverse corruption patterns.- The current state-of-the-art VCNet formulates corruption detection as a segmentation task to recognize corruption patterns. But it struggles with irregular corruptions and has limited generalization. This paper instead applies contrastive learning to learn intrinsic distinctions between corrupted and uncorrupted regions, improving generalization.- This paper proposes a hierarchical coarse-to-fine framework for contrastive learning to efficiently detect multi-scale corruption masks. This differs from prior contrastive learning methods that operate on full high-resolution images and can be computationally expensive.- The hierarchical framework enables semantic consistency and propagation of useful knowledge across scales. Special techniques like high-quality sample selection and inter-stage feature reusing are introduced.- The integrated blind image restoration model with hierarchical contrastive learning for detection and transformer-based synthesis outperforms specialized methods in inpainting and watermark removal.- Extensive experiments demonstrate the superior performance and generalization ability of the proposed method across different corruption patterns compared to VCNet and other approaches.In summary, this paper pushes the boundaries of blind image restoration by tackling more complex real-world corruptions. The novel hierarchical contrastive learning framework and integrated model advance the state-of-the-art in this field. The improved generalization is a notable advantage over prior arts.
