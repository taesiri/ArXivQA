# [From User Surveys to Telemetry-Driven Agents: Exploring the Potential of   Personalized Productivity Solutions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08960)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the paper:

This paper presents a two-phase study exploring user preferences for AI-based productivity agents and developing personalized solutions tailored to those preferences. 

In phase one, the authors conduct a survey with 363 information workers to understand their needs and desires for a productivity agent. Key findings show that participants feel most productive when making progress on tasks, helping others, and solving unexpected problems. Most want proactive suggestions from an agent that is knowledgeable, trustworthy and transparent. Top areas for assistance are meeting summaries, documentation, briefs, and retrieving information. Participants prefer moderate personalization balanced with privacy protections.

Based on the survey insights, in phase two the authors develop a productivity agent powered by GPT-4 that provides personalized advice using telemetry data on meetings, communication and collaboration gathered through Microsoft's Viva Insights platform. They compare this AI agent to alternative data presentation methods like dashboards and narratives in a study with 40 participants.

The AI agent has longer interaction times and provides more detailed responses than the other methods. Over 70% of participants agree it enhances personalization and empathy by using telemetry data. It performs well at avoiding negative traits and offering proactive, practical productivity suggestions. However, only 20% find it adaptable in conversation. 

Participants prefer the visual appeal and ease of dashboards combined with the personalized insights from the AI agent. The study highlights the importance of balancing personalization and privacy, designing for adaptability, and using LLMs to transform productivity data into actionable, empathetic advice. It contributes an understanding of user needs as well as a demonstration of how advanced AI can enhance productivity tools.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my understanding, the main contribution of this paper is a comprehensive two-phase study focused on understanding user preferences for productivity agents and designing personalized solutions tailored to these preferences. 

Specifically, the key contributions are:

1) Conducting an initial user survey with 363 participants to explore various aspects of productivity, communication style, agent approach, personality traits, personalization, and privacy. This provided insights into the desired characteristics and capabilities for a productivity agent.

2) Developing a custom application to facilitate user telemetry data sharing, enabling personalization of the AI-based productivity agent.

3) Designing and evaluating an AI productivity agent powered by GPT-4 and comparing its performance with alternative data presentation methods like dashboard and narrative. A study with 40 participants was conducted.

4) Providing design guidelines and recommendations to create more effective user-centric productivity solutions based on the empirical findings. For example, the importance of adaptability, balancing personalization and privacy, incorporating principles like progressive disclosure and semantic zooming.

5) Demonstrating the potential of large language models in transforming productivity insights delivery and interactions.

In summary, the key contribution is a comprehensive user-centric approach to understand preferences and develop personalized productivity solutions tailored to users' needs. The insights from the two-phase study can guide future research and development of productivity tools for information workers.
