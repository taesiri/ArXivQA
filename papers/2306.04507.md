# [Improving neural network representations using human similarity   judgments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.04507)

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

Based on my reading, the key points of this paper are:- The paper explores how aligning neural network representations with human similarity judgments impacts performance on downstream vision tasks like few-shot learning and anomaly detection. - Current representation learning objectives like supervised learning or contrastive learning do not directly constrain the global structure of learned representations. But human judgments of object similarity reflect global semantic structure, like superordinate categories.- The paper introduces a "gLocal transform" that aligns neural net representations with human judgments while preserving local structure. This transform improves performance on downstream tasks compared to the original representations.- The results suggest human visual representations have useful global structure that standard objectives fail to capture. Aligning neural nets with this structure via human judgments improves their ability to generalize.So in summary, the central hypothesis is that incorporating human judgments of global similarity structure into neural network representations can improve their transferability and generalization abilities for downstream tasks. The gLocal transform is introduced as a method for aligning global structure while preserving local relationships. The improved downstream performance supports the value of human-aligned global structure.
