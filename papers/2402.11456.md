# [FactPICO: Factuality Evaluation for Plain Language Summarization of   Medical Evidence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.11456)

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem: 
- Plain language summarization of medical evidence with large language models (LLMs) can improve accessibility of technical content, but there are concerns about factuality of generated summaries in such a high-stakes domain.
- There is no standard evaluation benchmark to assess factuality of LLM-generated plain language medical summaries.
- It is unknown whether existing automatic factuality metrics align with expert judgments in this domain. 

Proposed Solution:
- Present FactPICO, an expert-annotated dataset for evaluating factuality of LLM-generated plain language summaries of randomized controlled trial (RCT) abstracts.
- FactPICO focuses evaluation on key RCT elements - Populations, Interventions, Comparators, Outcomes (PICO) - and the factual reporting of results for these.
- Includes 345 summaries generated by GPT-4, Llama-2 and Alpaca from 115 RCT abstracts.
- Annotated with fine-grained ratings and free-text rationales by medical experts.
- Benchmark suite of automatic factuality metrics using FactPICO.

Key Findings:
- Factuality vs. simplicity tradeoff - more simplified summaries tended to introduce more errors.  
- Errors introduced regarding critical PICO elements and evidence inferences.
- Concerning rate of content additions that are non-factual (potentially misleading).
- Existing automatic metrics correlate poorly with expert judgments of factuality. 
- Providing explicit domain knowledge to metrics (e.g. identifying PICO spans) improves performance.

Main Contributions:
- First expert-annotated benchmark focused specifically on evaluating factuality of plain language summarization of medical evidence.
- Analysis highlighting remaining challenges for LLMs in balancing simplicity and factuality.
- Benchmarking of latest automatic factuality metrics.
- Dataset includes expert rationales supporting ratings to enable research into explainable factuality evaluation.
