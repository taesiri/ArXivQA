# [Exploring the Task-agnostic Trait of Self-supervised Learning in the   Context of Detecting Mental Disorders](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.15170)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Detecting mental disorders like major depressive disorder (MDD) and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) using behavioral data is challenging due to the heterogeneity within disorders and overlapping symptoms. 
- Finding a generic feature representation suitable for detecting multiple correlated mental disorders has not been explored.
- Publicly available datasets for developing detectors are limited.

Proposed Solution:
- Explore the task-agnostic traits of self-supervised learning (SSL) models for detecting MDD and PTSD using audio and video data. 
- Investigate two SSL models - PASE based on multi-target prediction and AALBERT based on masked prediction.
- Modify PASE by changing the prediction targets (PASE-mod) and generate global representations by varying stride sizes to capture temporal contexts.
- Apply average pooling on AALBERT outputs to obtain a global representation.
- Train SSL models on diverse datasets like DAIC-WOZ, IEMOCAP and LibriSpeech.

Key Contributions:
- PASE-mod provides improved detection over PASE, indicating usefulness of proposed prediction targets.
- Global representations from both PASE-mod and AALBERT give better detection than supervised baselines, exhibiting task-agnostic traits.
- Fine-tuning SSL models specifically for DAIC-WOZ dataset gives best individual disorder detection. 
- Combined dataset training generates representations that work reasonably well for both disorders, affirming task-agnostic traits.
- Study demonstrates applicability of SSL for mental disorder detection on both audio and video.

In summary, the paper explores SSL models to generate task-agnostic representations for detecting correlated mental disorders MDD and PTSD on audio-visual data. The global representations are shown to outperform supervised baselines while exhibiting trait-agnosticism.
