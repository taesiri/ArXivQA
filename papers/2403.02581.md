# [VEglue: Testing Visual Entailment Systems via Object-Aligned Joint   Erasing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.02581)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the paper:

Problem:
Visual entailment (VE) is an important multimodal reasoning task that determines the relationship between an image (premise) and a text sentence (hypothesis). Testing the reliability and robustness of VE systems is critical before deployment, but existing testing techniques have limitations: 1) They only perturb a single modality, breaking the semantic consistency between image and text, 2) They conduct shallow perturbations that can hardly affect the VE system's reasoning and have low issue-finding capability. 

Proposed Solution:
The paper proposes an object-aligned joint erasing approach called VEglue for testing VE systems. The key idea is to leverage objects as the basic reasoning components and conduct deeper joint erasing to images and texts based on object-level alignment:

1) It first detects objects in the image premise and extracts object descriptions from the text hypothesis. 

2) It then aligns the image objects with text descriptions to identify linked vs unlinked objects.

3) Based on the alignment, three Metamorphic Relations (MRs) are designed to erase: both the object and its linked text description, only linked objects, and only unlinked objects.

4) The modified image-text pairs are fed into the VE system, and errors are detected by checking if the predictions violate the MRs.

Main Contributions:

- Proposes a novel object-aligned joint erasing approach for testing VE systems by understanding semantic relations of image-text objects.

- Extensive experiments on 4 VE systems and 2 datasets show VEglue detects 11,609 issues on average and achieves 52.5% issue finding rate, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.

- Further model retraining using the detected issues improves accuracy on VEglue tests by 50.8% without sacrificing original accuracy.

- The idea of aligning and jointly erasing multimodal objects can motivate future research on testing multimodal AI systems.

In summary, the paper makes important contributions in reliability testing for the emerging and critical VE task by innovative object-aligned joint input perturbations.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contributions of this paper are:

1. It proposes an object-aligned joint erasing approach (named VEglue) for testing Visual Entailment systems, which is a representative multimodal reasoning task. VEglue generates tests by understanding the object-level semantic relations between the visual and textual modalities.

2. It evaluates VEglue on four widely-used VE systems and two datasets, and shows that VEglue can significantly outperform state-of-the-art baselines in terms of issue detection capability.

3. By retraining open-sourced VE models with the tests generated by VEglue, the accuracy on the new tests could be improved significantly without sacrificing accuracy on the original test set.

4. It provides insights into three types of issues detected in VE systems - similar objects misleading the system, nearby objects misleading the system, and semantically related objects misleading the system.

5. The authors release the implementation and datasets to facilitate reproducibility and future research.

In summary, the main contribution is proposing an effective object-aligned joint erasing approach for testing Visual Entailment systems, with comprehensive experimental evaluation to demonstrate its advantages. The released artifacts also make valuable contributions.


## What are the keywords or key terms associated with this paper?

 Based on my reading of the paper, some of the main keywords and key terms associated with it are:

- Visual entailment (VE) - The multimodal reasoning task that is the focus of the paper, involving predicting the relationship between an image premise and text hypothesis.

- Multimodal reasoning - Reasoning that involves comprehending and integrating information from multiple modalities like image and text. 

- Metamorphic testing - The testing framework used in the paper to generate new tests and oracles for the VE systems.

- Object-aligned joint erasing - The core technique proposed in the paper for testing VE systems, involving aligning and jointly erasing objects in the image premise and text hypothesis.

- Issue detection - Detecting errors or inconsistencies in the predictions of the VE systems using the metamorphic testing approach.

- Object detection - Identifying object regions in the image premises using detection models.  

- Visual grounding - Linking the detected object regions in premises to object descriptions in hypotheses.

- Metamorphic relations (MRs) - Transformations to generate new tests and oracles, three MRs are proposed based on object-aligned joint erasing.
