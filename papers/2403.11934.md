# [High-energy physics image classification: A Survey of Jet Applications](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.11934)

 Here is a detailed summary of the paper:

This paper provides a comprehensive survey on the application of machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) techniques for high-energy physics (HEP) jet image analysis. 

The paper first introduces key background information on HEP, including different particle types like W, Z and Higgs bosons, top quarks and b-jets. It also discusses performance metrics used to evaluate both classical and DL-based particle physics techniques. 

A taxonomy is then presented to represent jet images in HEP, covering available datasets from experiments like ATLAS and CMS, common preprocessing techniques like cropping, normalization and pixelization, and methods for feature extraction and selection from jet images.

The paper then explores the various DL architectures that have been proposed for HEP jet image analysis, including CNNs, RNNs, MLPs and graph neural networks. Key models like ParticleNet, DeepJet, LorentzGroupNetwork and equivariant graph neural networks are analyzed in detail regarding their architectures and performance on tasks like jet tagging, quark/gluon discrimination etc.  

A categorization of ML/DL-based jet image analysis techniques is provided, differentiating between quantum ML, CNN, adversarial training, RNN and other miscellaneous techniques. For each category, the latest state-of-the-art methods are reviewed, analyzing their working, experimental results and limitations.

The survey then shifts focus to applications of AI-based jet image analysis in areas like jet tagging, multi-jet classification, particle tracking, jet image generation etc. For each application, existing techniques are evaluated and future research directions are suggested. 

Finally, the paper summarizes the current status of DL for HEP jet analysis, highlighting key challenges around data quality, model interpretation and generalization. It concludes by identifying promising research avenues around novel DL architectures for HEP data and integration of domain knowledge to further advance the performance and applicability of DL techniques in this important scientific domain.
