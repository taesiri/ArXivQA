This demonstrates its utility.

4) SynFundus-1M is going to be publicly released to promote advancements in retinal disease diagnosis and medical imaging AI more broadly. This is expected to be a valuable contribution to the research community by alleviating data scarcity issues.

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Medical imaging datasets are scarce due to privacy restrictions, limiting the development of large models. This is especially true for retinal fundus images, which are important for diagnosing eye diseases.  
- Existing public fundus image datasets are small in size and have limited disease annotations. For example, the largest dataset AIROGS has 101k images labeled only for glaucoma.

Proposed Solution:
- The authors leverage a private dataset of 1.3 million annotated fundus images to train a generative model called SynFundus-Generator using latent diffusion models.
- The SynFundus-Generator is then used to synthesize a dataset called SynFundus-1M containing 1 million high quality synthetic fundus images with annotations for 15 eye diseases.

- An AI-assisted diagnostic system with NMPA certification is used to annotate both the real private images and SynFundus images.

Main Contributions:
- SynFundus-Generator model that can generate realistic and annotated synthetic fundus images.
- SynFundus-1M - the largest public fundus image dataset with 1 million synthetic images and annotations for 15 diseases.
- Experiments show CNNs and ViTs pre-trained on SynFundus-1M converge faster and outperform models pre-trained on ImageNet on downstream tasks like diabetic retinopathy and glaucoma diagnosis.
- Ophthalmologists find it very difficult to discern synthetic SynFundus images from real images.
- Quantitative evaluations using FID score demonstrate SynFundus-1M images resemble real images better than other synthetic datasets.

In summary, the paper proposes a solution to generate a large-scale synthetic fundus image dataset which can enable training better models for medical diagnosis tasks where data availability is limited due to privacy reasons.
