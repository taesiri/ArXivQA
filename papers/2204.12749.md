# Control Globally, Understand Locally: A Global-to-Local Hierarchical   Graph Network for Emotional Support Conversation

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

The central research question this paper addresses is how to effectively model the hierarchical relationships between the global cause, local psychological intentions and dialog history in emotional support conversation, in order to generate more supportive responses. Specifically, the paper proposes a novel model called Global-to-Local Hierarchical Graph Network (GLHG) to capture the global cause and local intentions and model their hierarchical relationships. The key ideas and components are:- Capturing global cause and local intentions: Use a multi-source encoder with COMET to extract the global cause describing the emotional problem and local psychological intentions of the help-seeker.- Modeling hierarchical relationships: Design a hierarchical graph reasoner to model the relationships between global cause (dialog-level), local intentions (sentence-level) and dialog history. - Training with global supervision: Propose a novel training objective to monitor the semantic information of global cause using the conversation's problem type, so that global cause will not be biased by local intentions.The central hypothesis is that by effectively modeling the hierarchical relationships between multi-source information (global, local and history), the model can gain better global-to-local understanding of the conversation and generate more supportive responses. Experiments on the ESConv dataset confirm the state-of-the-art performance of GLHG.

## Summarize the paper in one paragraph.

The goal is to generate supportive responses to a help-seeker by exploring the cause of their emotional distress and understanding their psychological intentions. ## Summarize the paper in two paragraphs.

Here is a two paragraph summary of the paper:The paper proposes a Global-to-Local Hierarchical Graph Network (GLHG) for emotional support conversation. The goal is to help reduce the emotional distress of the help-seeker by exploring the cause of their problem and understanding their psychological intentions. The GLHG model has three main components. First, a multi-source encoder captures the global cause of the problem, local psychological intentions, and dialog history. It uses a commonsense reasoning model called COMET to help identify intentions. Second, a hierarchical graph reasoner models relationships between the global cause, local intentions, and dialog history. This enables situational understanding. Third, a global-guide decoder generates supportive responses while monitoring semantic information about the global cause. This prevents the global understanding from becoming biased by local details. Experiments on the ESConv dataset show GLHG improves both automatic metrics and human evaluations compared to prior methods. It better identifies problems, provides comfort, and makes helpful suggestions.
