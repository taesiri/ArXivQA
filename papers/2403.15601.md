# [From Guidelines to Governance: A Study of AI Policies in Education](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.15601)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
With the increasing use of AI technologies like ChatGPT in education, there is a lack of clear policies and guidelines governing their ethical implementation. This creates risks around issues like student privacy, transparency, bias, and plagiarism. 

The paper investigates two key questions:
1) What is the current policy landscape regarding AI/LLMs in US educational institutions?  
2) What are administrators' perspectives on ideal policies and their key focus areas?

Methodology: 
The authors surveyed over 100 high school principals and higher education provosts in 5 US states using a mix of multiple choice and free-form questions to capture both quantifiable metrics and subjective viewpoints.

Key Findings:
- 80% of colleges are developing policies, but only 50% of high schools are. This indicates a policy gap between types of institutions.
- Most current policies are inadequate in coverage and fail to address risks unique to AI systems.  
- Administrators recognize the critical importance of developing specialized AI policies focused on ethics, plagiarism prevention and student safety.
- Suggested policy approaches emphasize flexible frameworks that can adapt quickly to evolving technologies.

Main Contributions:
- Documents the lack of adequate AI policies, especially in US high schools, and highlights key gaps like transparency and bias.
- Captures administrator attitudes showing support for urgent policy development governing ethical AI use.  
- Provides initial data and recommendations to guide policymakers in building effective and collaborative AI governance tailored to education.
- Underscores need for iterative policies that keep pace with AI advancement to ensure responsible integration.

The paper ultimately demonstrates the nascency of AI policies in US education and calls for developing adaptive and comprehensive ethical frameworks through multi-stakeholder participation.


## Summarize the paper in one sentence.

 This paper employs a survey of over 100 school administrators to examine the policy landscape and attitudes regarding the use of generative AI tools like ChatGPT in educational settings, finding a lack of adequate governance mechanisms despite widespread recognition of the need for ethical guidelines and iterative policy formulation.


## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contribution of this paper is providing an empirical understanding of the current policy landscape and attitudes regarding the use of generative AI tools like ChatGPT in educational settings. Specifically:

1) It reveals that while there is a push towards developing policies to govern AI use, especially in higher education, most existing policies are inadequate and fail to address key issues like algorithmic transparency and bias. High schools lag even further behind in policy development compared to higher ed.

2) It underscores the critical gaps that exist between the current state of policies and what is needed for the ethical integration of AI technologies like ChatGPT. Areas like privacy, accountability, and mitigating risks are not sufficiently covered.  

3) It highlights the perceived needs and recommendations for future policy development, emphasizing the importance of collaborative and adaptable governance models to keep pace with the rapid evolution of AI. Factors like involving multiple stakeholders, central oversight alongside autonomy, and iterative policy updating are advocated.

4) Overall, it establishes the necessity and urgency for developing comprehensive and evolving policies and guidelines to steer the use of AI tools in classrooms towards innovation while safeguarding ethical considerations. It calls for inclusive dialogues and strategic policy-making to balance emerging technologies' benefits and risks in education.

In summary, the paper's main contribution is providing original empirical insights into the policy gaps, attitudes, and needs related to regulating generative AI in US educational institutions, while advocating policy models to ethically harness AI's potential.
