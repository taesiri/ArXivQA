# [The Compute Divide in Machine Learning: A Threat to Academic   Contribution and Scrutiny?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.02452)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- There is a growing "compute divide" between industry and academic AI research labs in terms of access to compute resources for training machine learning models. Industry has much greater access to funding and engineering expertise to leverage large-scale compute.

- This compute divide coincides with a reduced representation of academic-only teams in compute-intensive research areas like large language models. Academic labs are being "sidelined" from research into the most advanced AI systems.

- The compute divide could have implications for diffusion and scrutiny of influential industry-developed AI models, as academia plays an important role in testing, validating and providing critical perspective on AI systems.

Proposed Solutions:
- The paper recommends policies to provide academic researchers privileged structured access to industry models for evaluation. 

- Government funding of shared national compute infrastructures is proposed to alleviate academic resource constraints. Compute allocation should prioritize scrutiny and safety research.

- Independent third-party auditing of industry AI systems is advised to complement academic scrutiny. A task force could research implementation approaches.

Main Contributions:
- Provides data analysis quantifying the compute divide between industry and academic AI research. 

- Argues compute divides will shape research agendas, with academia focusing more on industry-developed models and less on cutting-edge techniques.

- Discusses strategic implications concerning reduced diffusion and external scrutiny of influential industry AI models.

- Recommends policies around access, funding and auditing to ensure academic researchers can evaluate advanced models despite the compute divide.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my reading, the main contributions of this paper are:

1) It provides a data-driven analysis showing that there is a growing "compute divide" between industry and academic machine learning research. Specifically, it shows that industry labs are increasingly dominating the training of large-scale and compute-intensive machine learning models, while academic labs are becoming less represented.

2) It analyzes the strategic implications of this compute divide for the machine learning research ecosystem. Key implications discussed include:

- Shifts in the thematic composition of research between industry and academia, with industry more focused on areas that benefit from large-scale compute like large language models. 

- Potential sidelining of academic research from scrutinizing and evaluating large compute-intensive models like large language models. This could reduce critical analysis.

- Increased academic focus on using open-sourced models created by industry rather than training their own models from scratch.

3) It provides preliminary policy recommendations to help address issues arising from the compute divide, focused especially on ensuring scrutiny of large models. Key recommendations include privileged structured access, centralized compute provision, and third-party auditing.

In summary, the paper makes important contributions in empirically demonstrating the compute divide, analyzing the strategic implications it creates, and putting forward recommendations to help address resulting issues.
