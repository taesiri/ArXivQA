## What is the main contribution of this paper?

Based on my reading, the main contributions of this paper are:1. It proposes a Sparse Sampling Transformer with Uncertainty-Driven Ranking (UDR-S2Former) for unified removal of rain streaks and raindrops in images. 2. It introduces a Sparse Sampling Attention (SSA) module that can adaptively sample relevant image degradation information globally to model the relationships between rain streaks and raindrops.3. It leverages an uncertainty map to guide the feature learning in SSA via a novel uncertainty-driven ranking strategy. This helps focus on modeling degradation relationships. 4. It presents a Local Reconstruction (LR) module that uses the uncertainty map and its internal differences to help reconstruct locally degraded regions by extracting clean cues.5. Experiments show state-of-the-art performance of UDR-S2Former on multiple synthetic and real-world rainy image datasets containing diverse rain degradations. It outperforms previous specialized networks and universal restoration transformers.In summary, the key innovation is using the uncertainty map in a novel way to guide adaptive sparse sampling for degradation modeling and local reconstruction, allowing unified removal of rain streaks and raindrops of diverse shapes and sizes. The proposed modules bring flexibility and performance gains.

## Summarize the paper in one paragraph.

Here is a one paragraph summary of the paper:This paper proposes a new method called UDR-S$^{2}$Former for removing rain streaks and raindrops from images. The key ideas are 1) Using a sparse sampling attention mechanism to model long-range dependencies between rain degradations instead of fixed window attention. This allows capturing relationships between distant degradations like large raindrops and rain streaks. 2) Leveraging uncertainty maps to guide the attention to focus on degraded regions during sparse sampling. A ranking strategy is used on the uncertainty map to determine which areas need more attention. 3) Using the uncertainty map to also guide local reconstruction by directing attention to non-degraded areas to fill in missing information. Experiments show the method achieves state-of-the-art performance on rain removal benchmarks including both synthetic and real datasets. The approach is computationally efficient and outperforms previous window-based and global attention methods. Overall, the uncertainty-driven sparse sampling allows effectively modeling complex rain degradations for high quality removal.
