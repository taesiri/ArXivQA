# [Social Intelligence Data Infrastructure: Structuring the Present and   Navigating the Future](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14659)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- There is no standardized and comprehensive definition of social intelligence in AI systems. Prior work defines it narrowly by cognitive understanding or behavioral skills. 
- Existing social AI datasets are fragmented and focus on isolated aspects of social intelligence. There lacks a holistic perspective to understand gaps in current data landscape.

Proposed Solution:
- Develop a Social AI Data Infrastructure with two components:
   1) A taxonomy that hierarchically defines social intelligence across cognitive, situational and behavioral pillars.
   2) A library that maps 480 existing NLP datasets to taxonomy to organize current data efforts.

Main Contributions:  
- Taxonomy introduces structure and standardization to the concept of social intelligence.
- Data library enables analysis of distribution, trends and gaps of existing datasets.
- Analysis shows needs for more nuanced situations, multifaceted data, interactivity and diversity.
- Evaluation of language models using the taxonomy reveals limitations on nuanced tasks.
- Provided recommendations for future data collection, content, structure and ethics.

In summary, the paper proposes an infrastructure to organize current data landscape on social intelligence, support analysis to uncover gaps and emerging trends, benchmark language models' capabilities and limitations, and offer insights to guide future dataset development for advancing social intelligence in NLP systems.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 This paper's main contributions are:

1. Proposing a Social AI Taxonomy that comprehensively defines social intelligence in AI systems across three pillars - cognitive, situational, and behavioral intelligence. 

2. Constructing a data library of 480 NLP datasets related to social intelligence, mapped to the taxonomy categories to organize current data efforts.

3. Performing distributional and temporal analyses on the data library to highlight overlooked areas and uncover emerging trends. 

4. Evaluating large language models on taxonomy categories to understand current capabilities and limitations.

5. Providing recommendations for future social intelligence dataset development, including needs for multifaceted datasets, diversity, long-tail situations, interactivity, evolution, multimodality, representation, and ethics.

The taxonomy, data library, analyses, evaluations, and recommendations collectively introduce a Social Intelligence Data Infrastructure to structure the present landscape and navigate directions for advancing social intelligence in NLP.
