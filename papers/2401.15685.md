# [Assessment of Autism and ADHD: A Comparative Analysis of Drawing   Velocity Profiles and the NEPSY Test](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15685)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Differentiating between autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) can be challenging due to overlapping symptoms. Additional objective tools are needed to complement standardized assessments like the NEPSY-II test.  

- Motor impairments are common in ASD but have not been well studied. Understanding movement patterns could provide insights into underlying neuromotor signals and help distinguish ASD from ADHD.

Proposed Solution:
- Use a tablet application to record handwriting tasks and visualize velocity profiles. Compare results to scores from selected NEPSY-II subtests.

- Propose an observational scale to evaluate parameters of the velocity profiles: velocity pattern repetitions (VPR), velocity changes (VC), pauses (P) and time (T).

- Apply clustering algorithms on the velocity parameters to identify potential subgroups.

Experiment:
- 12 participants included - 6 with neurodevelopmental disorders (5 ASD, 1 ADHD) and 6 healthy controls.

- Performed 3 tasks: copying a circle, copying a square, writing "elelelel".

- Evaluated using NEPSY-II criteria and proposed velocity scale. 

Results:
- Differences more pronounced in velocity scale than NEPSY-II scores.

- Clustering distinguished an ASD subgroup, ADHD subject, and controls based on VPR and P.

- Findings highlight potential for additional velocity-based measures to complement standardized tests.

Contributions:
- First study to combine NEPSY-II and velocity profiles to differentiate ASD and ADHD.

- Proposed novel velocity observation scale to quantify movement patterns.

- Showed feasibility of identifying subgroups via velocity parameters.

Future Work: 
- Apply machine learning for automatic score calculation.
- Expand study with more participants.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my understanding, the main contribution of this paper is:

The paper proposes combining standardized tests from the NEPSY-II assessment with a new observational scale for assessing velocity profiles of movements. Specifically, it compares the results from the NEPSY-II visuospatial functions test on copying shapes with an analysis of velocity signals collected during the tasks. Four parameters are proposed for the velocity profile analysis - velocity pattern repetitions, velocity changes, pauses, and time.  

The goal is to gather additional neuromotor information to better differentiate between neurodevelopmental disorders like ASD and ADHD that can have overlapping symptoms and be challenging to distinguish clinically. The paper presents a proof-of-concept study on 12 subjects and shows that while NEPSY-II scores were largely similar between groups, the velocity profile analysis revealed differences between typical children and those with disorders, especially for subjects with ADHD.

In summary, the main contribution is demonstrating the potential of combining standardized visual-motor assessments with velocity profile observations to improve detection and diagnosis of neurodevelopmental disorders. The proposed velocity parameters could lead to new standardized measurement tools.
