# [NoiseDiffusion: Correcting Noise for Image Interpolation with Diffusion   Models beyond Spherical Linear Interpolation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.08840)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem: 
- Image interpolation with diffusion models is promising for creating interesting images, but current methods mainly rely on spherical linear interpolation in the latent space. 
- This works well for images generated by diffusion models, but faces challenges when interpolating natural images, often introducing artifacts.

- The authors analyze the process and find the core issue is that the encoding noise for natural images does not obey the expected normal distribution. This leads to invalid noise components that cause artifacts when denoised.

Proposed Solution - NoiseDiffusion:
- Correct the invalid noise to be closer to the normal distribution by adding subtle Gaussian noise and constraining extremes.
- This enhances image quality but can lose signal, so also inject the original images into the noisy versions before interpolation.

- Overall process:
    1. Encode images to latent space
    2. Add noise and constrain extremes 
    3. Interpolate the noisy versions 
    4. Inject original images into interpolated noisy versions
    5. Denoise to get final interpolated images

Main Contributions:
- Identified root cause of artifacts in interpolating natural images with diffusion models
- Proposed NoiseDiffusion method to correct invalid noise and retain signal
- Showed NoiseDiffusion enables effective interpolation of natural images without artifacts or information loss
- Established connections between NoiseDiffusion, spherical linear interpolation and other methods
- Demonstrated state-of-the-art interpolation performance on natural images

The key insight is to correct the invalid latent space noise while retaining the original signal, allowing high quality interpolation of natural images with diffusion models.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contribution of this paper is proposing a novel approach called NoiseDiffusion to correct noise for image interpolation with diffusion models. Specifically, NoiseDiffusion approaches invalid noise (that does not follow the expected distribution) to the desired distribution by introducing subtle Gaussian noise and suppressing noise with extreme values. This helps mitigate artifacts in image interpolation. At the same time, NoiseDiffusion performs interpolation in the noisy image space and injects raw images to address the issue of information loss. As a result, NoiseDiffusion enables interpolating natural images without causing artifacts or losing original image information, thus achieving superior interpolation performance compared to existing methods like spherical linear interpolation and directly introducing noise.
