# [Depression Detection on Social Media with Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.10750)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Depression causes significant harm, but many patients don't seek diagnosis and treatment due to stigma or lack of awareness, leading to poor outcomes. 
- Depression detection on social media aims to determine if someone suffers from depression by analyzing their posts, enabling early intervention.
- Key challenges are (1) requiring expert medical knowledge and (2) needing high accuracy and explainability.

Proposed Solution - DORIS System
- Incorporates medical knowledge (DSM-5 depression criteria) into analysis using a large language model (LLM) to annotate high-risk texts.  
- Models users' mood courses over time by filtering emotionally intense posts and summarizing the mood history.
- Combines LLM-based features, mood course features and post embeddings as input to a classifier for accurate predictions.
- Generates explanations using LLM to describe identified symptoms and mood history to explain predictions.

Main Contributions:
- Novel depression detection system integrating professional medical knowledge from DSM-5 with advanced large language models.  
- System design combining different components to achieve both high accuracy through classifier and high explainability through LLM outputs.
- Extensive experiments validate accuracy improvements over baselines, effectiveness of designed components, and quality of generated explanations.

In summary, this paper proposes the DORIS system to advance depression detection on social media by harnessing medical knowledge and modern NLP techniques, specifically large language models, to enable accurate yet interpretable automated diagnosis. The thoughtful system design and thorough evaluation demonstrate DORIS's ability to reliably detect depression cases while also explaining its reasoning.
