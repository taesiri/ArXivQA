## Summarize the main method used in the paper in one paragraph.

Here is a one paragraph summary of the main method used in the paper:The paper proposes a framework called Adversarial Camouflage for Transferable and Intensive Vehicle Evasion (ACTIVE) for generating adversarial camouflage textures that can conceal 3D vehicles from detectors. The key aspects of the method are: 1) It uses triplanar mapping, a sophisticated texture mapping technique, to apply a common camouflage texture to different 3D vehicle surfaces in an object-independent manner. 2) It introduces a novel stealth loss function that minimizes detection scores from all valid classes to render the vehicle undetectable instead of just misclassified. 3) It uses a neural texture renderer to generate photo-realistic adversarial textures while maintaining differentiability for white-box attacks. 4) It incorporates random output augmentation and smooth/camouflage losses to enhance robustness, naturalness and background blending. Experiments show the method can consistently evade detection on various models and exhibits universality by transferring across vehicles, classes, tasks, and to the real world.
