# [NeRFTAP: Enhancing Transferability of Adversarial Patches on Face   Recognition using Neural Radiance Fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.17332)

## Write a very high-quality paragraph summarizing the paper that effectively covers the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary paragraph of the key points from the paper:

This paper proposes a novel adversarial attack method on face recognition called NeRFTAP that enhances the transferability of adversarial patches to both the target face recognition model and the victim's face images. Leveraging NeRF-based 3D-GAN, the method generates new view face images of the source and target subjects to improve patch relocatability simultaneously on the model and image. A style consistency loss is introduced to ensure visual similarity between the adversarial and target UV maps with a 0-1 mask, making the attack more effective and imperceptible. Experiments demonstrate NeRFTAP’s superior attack success rates over state-of-the-art methods on various face recognition models like ArcFace and systems like Face++. The results highlight the practical threat of the attack's direct transferability to victims' face images beyond just the model. The method provides valuable insights into enhancing robustness against such real-world adversarial attacks on face recognition systems. Through both model and image relocatability as well as style consistency, NeRFTAP advances the state-of-the-art in black-box impersonation attacks for improved security of face recognition technologies.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 According to the paper, the main contributions are:

1. Proposing NeRFTAP, a novel adversarial attack method on face recognition that considers transferability to both the FR model and victim's face images.

2. For the first time, utilizing NeRF-based 3D-GAN to simultaneously improve the relocatability of adversarial patches on FR models and victim's face images. 

3. Introducing a style consistency loss to ensure visually similar adversarial face images, making the attacks more effective and practical in real-world scenarios.  

4. Demonstrating the efficacy of the proposed approach through extensive experiments and evaluations on various FR models, highlighting its superiority over existing attack techniques.

In summary, the key contribution is proposing the NeRFTAP method that can generate more transferable and effective adversarial patches by leveraging 3D-GAN while also ensuring visual realism, leading to more practical and threatening attacks on face recognition systems.
