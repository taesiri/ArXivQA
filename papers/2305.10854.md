# [3D Registration with Maximal Cliques](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10854)

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

The main research question addressed in this paper is how to perform accurate 3D point cloud registration in the presence of a large number of outlier correspondences. Specifically, the paper proposes a new method called MAC (3D Registration with Maximal Cliques) to estimate the optimal 6 degree-of-freedom rigid transformation between two point clouds. The key idea is to use the maximal clique constraint to generate precise pose hypotheses from initial noisy correspondences.The main hypothesis is that by loosening the previous maximum clique constraint and mining more local consensus information in the correspondence graph, the proposed MAC method can achieve better registration performance compared to prior art, especially in challenging cases with heavy outliers and low overlap. The experiments aim to demonstrate MAC's effectiveness in terms of registration accuracy across different datasets and its ability to boost existing deep learning based methods.In summary, the core research question is how to achieve robust 3D point cloud registration from noisy correspondences. The key hypothesis is that using maximal cliques rather than maximum cliques to generate pose hypotheses can capture more accurate local consensus and lead to improved performance. The experiments verify this hypothesis and demonstrate MAC's state-of-the-art results.


## What is the main contribution of this paper?

Based on my reading, the main contributions of this paper are:1. It proposes a new 3D point cloud registration method called MAC (3D registration with Maximal Cliques) that uses maximal cliques in a compatibility graph to generate accurate pose hypotheses. 2. MAC loosens the tight maximum clique constraint used in prior works and mines more local consensus information to generate better hypotheses.3. Experiments on various datasets including U3M, 3DMatch, 3DLoMatch, and KITTI show MAC achieves state-of-the-art performance. It outperforms many geometric and learning based methods.4. MAC can be combined with existing deep learning frameworks as a plugin module to further boost their performance in a cross-dataset setting. For example, MAC + GeoTransformer achieves 95.7%/78.9% registration recall on 3DMatch/3DLoMatch.5. The paper provides detailed ablation studies analyzing the impact of different components in MAC like graph construction, clique selection, hypothesis evaluation etc. In summary, the key novelty is using maximal rather than maximum cliques to generate better hypotheses for 3D point cloud registration, demonstrated through comprehensive experiments and analysis. MAC is a simple yet effective geometric method that advances the state-of-the-art.
