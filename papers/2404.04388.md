# [Mining Potentially Explanatory Patterns via Partial Solutions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.04388)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Genetic algorithms (GAs) are able to solve complex optimization problems but act as black boxes, making it hard for users to trust or understand the solutions. This lack of explainability is an issue, especially for high-stake applications.

- Combinatorial optimization problems are complex despite their simple construction and are often approached with metaheuristics like GAs. But the internal mechanisms of GAs are difficult for humans to understand, needing explainability techniques.

Solution: 
- The paper introduces the concept of "Partial Solutions" (PSs) - sub-configurations of parameters associated with high fitness solutions, representing beneficial traits. 

- A PS catalog called the "PS basis" is generated by analyzing a reference population to find optimal PSs based on simplicity, mean fitness, and atomicity.

- The PS basis provides global explanations by finding key PSs and local explanations by showing which PSs exist in a solution.

- PSs also act as a model for generating new solutions via a "pick and merge" algorithm, maintaining search ability.

Contributions:

- Defines metrics (simplicity, mean fitness, atomicity) to characterize PSs suited for explainability.

- Proposes a system to find a PS basis from a reference population and use it to generate solutions.

- Introduces an archive-based search method to efficiently find PS catalogs. 

- Shows the approach can solve problems comparably or better than a GA while adding explainability.

- Provides both global explanations of the fitness landscape and local explanations of specific solutions.

- Demonstrates success at finding target PSs in Royal Road/Overlaps and Trap K benchmark problems.

In summary, the paper offers a novel explainability technique via Partial Solutions that reveals insights into solutions produced by GAs while maintaining or improving optimization ability.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my reading of the paper, the main contribution is proposing the concept of Partial Solutions (PSs) to improve the explainability of solutions found by genetic algorithms (GAs) for combinatorial optimization problems. Specifically:

- PSs represent beneficial sub-patterns or traits that are common in high-fitness solutions. They provide a way to explain why a particular solution is good by decomposing it into its constituent PSs.

- A PS mining algorithm is presented that can find a set of simple, high-fitness, and non-redundant PSs (called the PS basis) from a reference population. This provides a global explanation of the fitness landscape. 

- The set of PSs also serves as an explicit probabilistic model that can be sampled to generate new candidate solutions competitive with a standard GA. So the PSs assist the search process in addition to explainability.

- Experiments on benchmark problems demonstrate the ability of the approach to find target PSs, scale to larger reference populations, solve problems comparably or better than a GA/UMDA, and consistently find global optima.

In summary, the key novelty is using PSs, found by searching over the space of sub-solutions, to provide local and global explainability while also helping solve the optimization problem - improving understandability without compromising performance.
