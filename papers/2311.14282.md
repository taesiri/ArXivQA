 Here is a high-quality paragraph summarizing the key points of the paper:

This paper proposes a novel method to enhance image super-resolution (SR) by introducing text prompts as additional priors. The authors develop a pipeline to integrate text degradation descriptions into an SR dataset using discretization and binning techniques. They propose PromptSR, which employs a diffusion model conditioned on low-resolution images and text embeddings from a frozen pre-trained language model, to perform text-guided SR. PromptSR is trained on the text-image dataset generated by the pipeline. Experiments demonstrate that providing textual degradation information as prompts consistently improves SR results over state-of-the-art methods on both synthetic and real-world benchmarks. The discrete textual representation retains flexibility while aiding the network to model complex degradations. By enabling text guidance, PromptSR restores higher visual quality images with fewer artifacts. This work shows the promise of using text prompts for conditional image generation and paves the way for further multi-modal image restoration research.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contribution of this paper is introducing text prompts to provide degradation priors for enhancing image super-resolution. Specifically, the authors:

1) Develop a text-image generation pipeline to integrate text prompts into the SR dataset through a text degradation representation and a degradation model. 

2) Propose the PromptSR network to realize text prompt super-resolution based on a diffusion model and pre-trained language model. The text prompts are embedded and injected into the diffusion model to guide the image reconstruction process.

3) Demonstrate through extensive experiments that introducing text prompts significantly improves super-resolution performance on both synthetic and real-world datasets compared to state-of-the-art single modal methods.

In summary, the key contribution is using text prompts as an additional modality to provide useful degradation priors for advancing image super-resolution quality and performance.


## What are the keywords or key terms associated with this paper?

 Based on my review of the paper, some of the key terms and concepts associated with this work include:

- Image super-resolution (SR): The core task that the paper focuses on, which is recovering a high-resolution image from a low-resolution image.

- Text prompts: The use of natural language text to provide additional information and priors to guide the image SR process. This is the key idea proposed in the paper. 

- Degradation priors: The text prompts provide information about the degradation process that generated the low-resolution images. This aids in reconstruction.

- Text-image dataset: The authors generate a dataset that pairs low/high-resolution images with corresponding text prompts describing the degradation.

- Diffusion model: A probabilistic generative model that is utilized, along with a text encoder, to perform the text-conditioned image SR.

- Pre-trained language model: Models like CLIP and T5 that encode the text prompts into useful representations for conditioning the diffusion model.

- Denoising network: The diffusion model uses a denoising neural network that reverses the noise added to images during sampling.
