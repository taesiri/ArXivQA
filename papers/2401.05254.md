# [Language-based Valence and Arousal Expressions between the United States   and China: a Cross-Cultural Examination](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.05254)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Affective expressions have been extensively studied but primarily focused on the Western context. There are substantial cultural differences that contribute to affective expressions. 
- Past cross-cultural comparisons relied on small lab samples with self-reports. There is a need to examine actual language behaviors at scale across cultures.

Objectives:  
- Compare the functional relationship between valence (pleasantness) and arousal (activation) in public affective expressions on social media between the US and China. 
- Use language samples to elucidate differences and similarities in the valence-arousal relationship across the two cultures.

Methods:
- Collected large-scale Twitter (US) and Weibo (China) posts. Matched samples by gender and age distributions.
- Measured valence and arousal using existing lexicons translated between English and Mandarin. 
- Tested six models for the valence-arousal relationship. Used mixed effects models to capture user variations.   
- Identified language markers (n-grams, topics) associated with valence and arousal in each culture.

Key Findings:
- Confirm an asymmetrical V-shaped relationship between valence and arousal in both cultures.
- American participants showed stronger negativity bias and higher overall arousal. 
- Language markers indicate US expressions more about personal life while China about socio-political issues.
- US users employ explicit excitement terms while China users express positivity more implicitly.

Main Contributions:
- First large-scale confirmation of an affective structure relationship across cultures based on actual language use rather than self-reports.
- Elucidation of cultural differences in ideal and expressed affect between the US and China.
- Novel empirical evidence regarding theories of universal and cultural-specific aspects of affect.
