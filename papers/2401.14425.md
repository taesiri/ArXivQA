# [No Longer Trending on Artstation: Prompt Analysis of Generative AI Art](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.14425)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

The paper analyzes over 3 million text prompts and associated AI-generated images to understand how people are using text-to-image (TTI) systems like Stable Diffusion and Midjourney. The goal is to study the impact of these systems on visual art and culture, especially regarding how artistic concepts are represented in prompt language.

The paper compares prompting datasets from mid-2022 to late 2023 to see how usage has evolved as TTI systems have become more popular. Analysis methods include natural language processing, topic modeling, and image captioning.

Key findings:
- Prompting focuses heavily on surface aesthetics like image quality, lighting, and viewpoint rather than deeper artistic concepts. This may limit creativity.
- Popular artists and styles dominate prompts, raising concerns around copyright, style theft, and artistic voice. Prolific AI mimicry could reinforce the prominence of certain styles.  
- Topic analysis shows a shift from niche topics like fantasy art towards more mainstream art styles as user base diversifies over time.
- Image captioning reveals most common subjects are young women and anime characters. Output reinforces existing gender and cultural biases.

In conclusion, current TTI systems promote stylistic and topical conformity rather than innovation. Surface-level mimicry of style does not constitute artistic practice. The paper argues AI art is unlikely a serious threat to human art at this point.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my reading of the paper, the main contribution is an analysis of over 3 million text prompts and associated AI-generated images to understand how people are using language to create images with text-to-image systems. Specifically, the paper:

- Compares prompting datasets from mid-2022 to late 2023 to see how usage has evolved as the systems have become more popular and sophisticated over time
- Analyzes the prompts using NLP and visualization methods to identify topics, popular artists referenced, emphasis on surface aesthetics, etc. 
- Aims to understand the impact of these AI image generation systems on visual art and culture, including issues around bias, stereotypes, style theft, and lack of diversity
- Finds that prompting focuses largely on surface aesthetics and reinforcing cultural norms and popular styles rather than unique voices or narratives
- Concludes that while generative AI has produced a vast number of images, most uses seem recreational rather than truly artistic, and the images lack the authenticity and memorability of human-created art

In summary, the main contribution is using prompt analysis to gain a broader understanding of how generative AI is influencing visual art and culture through the language and images it promotes.
