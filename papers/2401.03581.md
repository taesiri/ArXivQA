## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Mindfulness meditation is beneficial for mental health, but adherence is challenging without guided in-person practice. Technology-based methods like mobile apps, conversational agents, and socially assistive robots can improve accessibility, but limited ability to generate real-time personalized content is a key limitation.  
- High-quality text-to-speech (TTS) voices are needed to deliver personalized mindfulness guidance and support, but TTS voices have not been evaluated for emotional expressiveness required for mindfulness. 
- Effects of physical embodiment and personalization on user perception of TTS voices for mindfulness are unknown.

Methods:
- Two-phase human subject study to evaluate TTS voices for mindfulness and effects of embodiment and personalization.
- Phase 1 (N=471 online): Between-subjects study comparing 3 TTS voices to 2 human voices across 3 embodiments. 
- Phase 2 (N=94 in-person): Within-subjects study comparing best TTS voice, best human voice and user-personalized TTS voice.

Key Findings:
- Best human voice outperformed all TTS voices, but TTS voices performed similarly to lower-rated human voice. Users satisfied with TTS clarity, but emotional expressiveness and naturalness were rated poorly.  
- Physical embodiment negatively impacted one TTS voice, unexpectedly amplifying perceived artificial sound. Servomotor sounds also negatively impacted TTS voice ratings.
- User-personalized TTS voices outperformed non-personalized TTS, and performed similarly to best human voice, suggesting personalization is a highly effective way to improve TTS quality.

Contributions:
- First evaluation of TTS voices for delivering mindfulness interventions 
- Demonstration of effects of embodiment and personalization on TTS voice perception
- Evidence that TTS personalization can greatly improve user-perceived quality to near human levels

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 Based on my reading of the paper, the main contribution is:

The paper presents a two-phase human subject study that evaluated and compared the user-perceived quality of state-of-the-art text-to-speech (TTS) voices versus human therapists' voices for delivering mindfulness meditation instructions. The study considered three different physical embodiments (no agent, conversational agent, and socially assistive robot) and also explored the effect of personalizing TTS voices based on user preferences. The key findings are:

1) All TTS voices were rated significantly worse than the best human therapist's voice for mindfulness meditation, though they performed similarly to a lower-rated human voice. 

2) Physical embodiment negatively impacted the rating of one TTS voice when embodied in a robot, suggesting the alignment of the robotic embodiment and TTS voice amplified dislike of the artificial voice. 

3) User-personalized TTS voices outperformed non-personalized TTS and performed almost as well as the best human voice, suggesting personalization is an effective way to improve TTS quality.

In summary, the main contribution is the two-phase study comparing TTS and human voices for mindfulness across embodiments and demonstrating the benefit of TTS voice personalization.
