# [Boosting Adversarial Transferability across Model Genus by   Deformation-Constrained Warping](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03951)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the paper:

Problem:
- Generating adversarial examples that can transfer across different model architectures (especially across different model genera like CNNs and Transformers) is challenging due to the huge architecture gap. 
- Existing methods for improving transferability mainly focus on augmenting the global content and neglect the importance of local details.

Proposed Solution: 
- The paper proposes a novel input transformation approach called Deformation-Constrained Warping (DeCoW) to increase the diversity of local details while preserving global semantics.

- DeCoW first applies an elastic deformation to the input using a warping transformation with random noise. This increases local shape and contour diversity.

- To prevent severe distortion of global semantics, an adaptive control strategy is used to constrain the strength and direction of the warping by optimizing the noise.

- DeCoW is integrated into the MI-FGSM attack framework to create the Deformation-Constrained Warping Attack (DeCoWA).

Main Contributions:
- Systematically investigate cross model genus adversarial attacks between CNNs and Transformers. Reveal importance of manipulating local structure for transferability.

- Propose novel DeCoW technique to increase local detail diversity while preserving global semantics via constrained elastic deformations.

- Achieve state-of-the-art attack transferability between CNNs and Transformers on image, video and audio tasks, significantly outperforming prior arts.

- Establish a strong baseline for future research on cross-model genus adversarial attacks.

In summary, the key innovation is the DeCoW technique to manipulate local details in a constrained manner to improve adversarial transferability across different model genera like CNNs and Transformers. Extensive experiments verify state-of-the-art performance.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contributions of this paper can be summarized as:

1. The paper systematically investigated the task of cross model genus attack and revealed that the low transferability of adversarial samples generated by previous attack methods is due to insufficient manipulation on the local structure of the signal.

2. The paper proposed a generic Deformation-Constrained Warping (DeCoW) to boost the adversarial transferability across model genera. DeCoW can increase the diversity of local details such as local shape and contours through elastic deformation and adaptively constrains the magnitude and direction of warping transformation. 

3. The paper proposed Deformation-Constrained Warping Attack (DeCoWA), which integrates DeCoW into a gradient-based attack method. DeCoWA is an approach that can be applied to various modalities of data such as image, video, and audio, and achieves superior transferability over the state-of-the-art attack methods.

In summary, the main contribution is the proposal of the DeCoW technique and DeCoWA attack to significantly improve adversarial transferability across different model genera, which is a challenging but important problem.


## What are the keywords or key terms associated with this paper?

 Based on my reading of the paper, some of the key terms and keywords associated with it are:

- Cross model genus attack - Transferring adversarial examples across different model architectures or genera, like between CNNs and Transformers. A key focus of the paper.

- Adversarial transferability - The ability of adversarial examples crafted on one model to fool other models. Needed for black-box attacks.

- Deformation-Constrained Warping (DeCoW) - The proposed input transformation method to augment images/videos/audio while preserving semantics. 

- Elastic deformation - Deforming inputs like images in a flexible way to create more diverse local details. DeCoW is based on this.

- Local details - Lower-level patterns like edges and textures that CNNs rely on more than Transformers. DeCoW increases local detail diversity.  

- Global semantics - Higher-level content and meaning of the input. DeCoW aims to preserve this while transforming local details.

- Deformation-Constrained Warping Attack (DeCoWA) - The overall attack algorithm integrating DeCoW to craft adversarial examples.

- Model genera - Groups of models with similar architectures, like CNNs or Transformers. The key distinction DeCoWA aims to bridge.

The core ideas focus on improving adversarial transferability, especially between different model genera via input transformations like the proposed DeCoW method.
