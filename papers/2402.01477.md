# [A Modular Aerial System Based on Homogeneous Quadrotors with   Fault-Tolerant Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.01477)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Standard quadrotors are limited in their ability to generate desired forces/torques in 6 degrees of freedom (DOF) due to their underactuated dynamics. This limits their usefulness for complex aerial tasks requiring physical interaction.
- Existing modular aerial systems compromise between mechanical complexity and controllable DOF. There is a need for a system with mechanical simplicity like a quadrotor, but more controllable DOF.

Proposed Solution: 
- The paper proposes "IdentiQuad", a modular aerial system based on homogeneous quadrotor modules that can be assembled to generate up to 6 controllable DOF.  
- Each IdentiQuad module has a standard quadrotor frame with an attached cross-shaped frame and four identical connectors. The connectors allow rigid mounting between modules at different relative angles.
- An assembly of n IdentiQuads is modeled as a rigid body and its dynamics are derived using Newton-Euler formalism. A general controller is proposed for trajectory tracking that balances energy use between modules. 
- A fault-tolerant control method is introduced to maintain controllability after in-flight rotor failures by reducing tracking DOF.

Main Contributions:
- Design of a modular aerial system called IdentiQuad that uses only homogeneous quadrotor modules but can generate up to 6 controllable DOF based on its configuration.
- A general controller for IdentiQuad assemblies that balances the energy consumption of individual modules to maximize flight time.
- A fault-tolerant control strategy that maintains a safe degree of controllability during rotor failures by strategically reducing position/orientation tracking DOF.
- Physics-based simulations demonstrating 6-DOF trajectory tracking, rotor fault tolerance, and extended flight times for different IdentiQuad assemblies.

In summary, the key innovation is the design of a modular aerial system with simplicity of quadrotors but versatility of assembled configurations and robust controls. Simulations verify the potential for complex aerial interactions.

2. It proposes a general controller suitable for different assembly configurations that balances energy consumption between modules and maximizes overall operating time.

3. It proposes a fault-tolerant control strategy to tolerate rotor failures and maintain controllability of the assembly.

In summary, the key contributions are:

- A reconfigurable and modular aerial system using standard quadrotors as building blocks
- A control strategy for trajectory tracking that balances energy use and handles rotor faults
- Validating the system's capabilities and controller performance through physics-based simulations

The modular design, generalized controller, and fault tolerance aim to improve versatility, robustness and reliability compared to standard quadrotor systems.
