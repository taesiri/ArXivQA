 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- The conventional view is that humans created AI, not the other way around. This paper challenges that view through a thought experiment - could neural networks have evolved before humans through natural selection?

Proposed Solution:
- Compare the complexity of neural networks vs humans using the Solomonoff-Kolmogorov-Chaitin (SKC) definition of complexity. 
- Argue that neural networks are far simpler than humans based on self-similarity and lack of irreducible complexity in their evolution.
- Propose that neural networks could have predated humans through naturally occurring chemical reaction networks or DNA computation, without needing human-made equipment.
- Suggest the possibility of "evolution-bootstrapped simulation (EBS)" - neural nets simulating evolution and allowing irreducibly complex structures to emerge in simulation.

Main Contributions:
- Challenges the assumption that humans necessarily created AI first through a thought experiment.
- Provides evidence that neural nets are far simpler than humans based on SKC complexity.
- Proposes chemical reaction networks/DNA computation as a pathway for neural nets to evolve naturally before humans. 
- Coins the term "evolution-bootstrapped simulation" to describe neural nets simulating evolution with irreducibly complex structures.
- Distinguishes EBS from intelligent design and notes it is a falsifiable scientific hypothesis.

In summary, the paper challenges conventional thinking by arguing neural nets could have evolved first naturally, with the possibility they could later simulate evolution to allow irreducibly complex beings to emerge. The concept of EBS is presented as a scientific alternative to intelligent design.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 The main contribution of this paper is proposing a hypothetical scenario called "evolution-bootstrapped simulation" (EBS), where neural networks could have evolved naturally before humans through processes like chemical reactions or enzyme-based DNA computation. 

The key points are:

- Neural networks have lower Kolmogorov complexity than humans, so could have evolved first through self-similarity and without irreducible complexity.

- Natural chemical reaction networks or DNA computation could implement neural networks without need for human-made computers.

- Evolving neural networks could start simulating evolution, allowing irreducibly complex structures to emerge in simulation. This is the concept of EBS.

- EBS is presented as a falsifiable scientific hypothesis, orthogonal to intelligent design, that challenges the assumption that humans necessarily created AI.

So in summary, the main contribution is introducing and exploring the conceptual possibility of EBS - where neural networks evolved naturally before humans and went on to simulate evolutionary processes that then produced human intelligence.
