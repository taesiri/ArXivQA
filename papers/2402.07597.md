# [Trustworthy SR: Resolving Ambiguity in Image Super-resolution via   Diffusion Models and Human Feedback](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.07597)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the paper:

Problem: 
Single image super-resolution (SR) is an ill-posed inverse problem where a single low-resolution (LR) input can correspond to multiple feasible high-resolution (HR) outputs. This introduces ambiguity in reconstructing a unique solution. Recent diffusion models can generate multiple plausible SR solutions that cover the solution space well, but selecting a single trustworthy output becomes challenging, especially when extracting critical information.  

Proposed Solution:
The authors propose a human feedback-based approach to resolve ambiguity and obtain a reliable SR solution from the samples generated by diffusion models. Specifically:

1) A pre-trained latent diffusion model (LDM) is used to generate a diverse set of SR samples given an LR input. 

2) Human subjects are asked to select up to 5 most likely/natural looking samples.

3) The selected samples are ensembled by pixel-wise averaging to obtain a single SR output.

This leverages diffusion models' ability to spanning the SR space, while using human perception to determine trustworthiness.

Main Contributions:

1) A new approach to obtain a single trustworthy SR solution from the ambiguous outputs of diffusion models using human feedback.

2) Introduction of a sample selection and ensembling framework that can work with any pre-trained diffusion model to improve reliability.

3) Experimental demonstration that the proposed LDM with Strategic Sample selection (LDM-SS) outperforms state-of-the-art SR methods in resolving ambiguity and generating visually appealing outputs, even though quantitative metrics may not reflect such improvements.

In summary, the key idea is to use human perception to guide diffusion model sampling towards reliable and detailed SR reconstructions that resolve ambiguity. The proposed methodology is model-agnostic and improves visual quality and trustworthiness.
