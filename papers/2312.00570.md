# [Generative models for visualising abstract social processes: Guiding   streetview image synthesis of StyleGAN2 with indices of deprivation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.00570)

## Write a very high-quality paragraph summarizing the paper that effectively covers the paper.

 Here is a summary paragraph of the paper:

This paper presents a novel application of generative adversarial networks (GANs) to study visual aspects of social processes and inequality in cities. A StyleGAN2 model was trained on a custom dataset of 14,564 Google Street View images from London. The images were inverted to find latent representations that were compared across three inversion techniques. Each image was associated with socioeconomic metadata from the Indices of Multiple Deprivation, allowing the mapping of visual features related to income, health, and environmental quality within the latent space. New synthetic images could then be generated by conditioning along directions encoding deprived versus privileged areas. The resulting visualizations uncover distinct architectural styles, public space usages, and streetscape elements tied to abstract concepts like education and income levels. Despite limitations in dataset bias and validation, the generative framework shows promise for gaining visual insights into urban inequality and repurposing GANs as part of social scientific methodology.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Social scientists and urban planners have long studied how urban landscapes reflect and perpetuate social inequalities, but many aspects are not easily captured numerically. 
- Recent work has used street-level images to predict non-visual city attributes, but the visual features driving the predictions are often not interpretable.

Proposed Solution:
- Train a StyleGAN2 generative model on 14,564 Google Street View images from London to synthesize realistic street-level views.
- Invert the training images to find latent codes corresponding to them. Associate codes with socioeconomic deprivation data from the area where the photo was taken.
- Identify directions in latent space corresponding to visual features associated with income, health, and education deprivation. 
- Generate new views by traversing the latent space along those directions to visualize differences in street appearance corresponding to those factors.

Main Contributions:
- Novel application of GANs to study unknown visual correlates of abstract social processes related to inequality.
- Technique to condition image generation on external statistical data about income, health, and education deprivation levels.
- Analysis of resulting synthetic images reveals systematic differences in architecture, greenery, etc. between more and less deprived areas.
- Interactive interface for users to guide image synthesis along deprivation dimensions using sliders.
- Demonstrates potential for generative models to be incorporated into social scientific methods to render tangible the visual aspects of complex societal phenomena.
