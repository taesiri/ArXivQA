# [GaitGCI: Generative Counterfactual Intervention for Gait Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03428)

## What is the central research question or hypothesis that this paper addresses?

Based on my reading of the paper, the central research question is: How can we design a gait recognition system that focuses on discriminative regions representing walking patterns, rather than being misled by confounding factors like viewpoints or clothing? The key hypothesis is that using generative counterfactual intervention and diversity-constrained dynamic convolution will allow the model to ignore confounding factors and focus on regions that effectively capture gait patterns, thus improving performance.In particular, the paper proposes a framework called GaitGCI that has two main components:1) Counterfactual intervention learning (CIL) - Uses causal inference concepts to analyze the direct relationship between the network's attention and its prediction, ignoring confounding factors. 2) Diversity-constrained dynamic convolution (DCDC) - Generates adaptive sample-specific factual and counterfactual attention maps to capture distinctive properties of each input sample.The central hypothesis is that by using CIL to minimize the impact of confounders and having DCDC generate tailored attention maps, GaitGCI will focus on discriminative regions representing gait patterns, rather than being misled by confounding factors. This should allow it to achieve state-of-the-art performance on gait recognition across diverse scenarios.
