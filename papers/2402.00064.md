# [Merging plans with incomplete knowledge about actions and goals through   an agent-based reputation system](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.00064)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem:
- Transition planning is a major challenge for people with cognitive disabilities like autism. Finding automated ways to generate transition plans would be very helpful for this community. 
- Multi-agent planning has been studied before, but typically relies on complex domain-dependent knowledge representation about plans. This paper explores a different approach.

Proposed Solution: 
- An agent-based system where "operator" agents generate plans and "node" agents execute them. Operator agents can request plan recommendations from each other.  
- Operator agents merge recommended plans from others with their own previous plans to generate an improved plan. The merging relies on the past execution results of plans, not an analysis of the plan structure itself.
- Four different algorithms are proposed for merging plans using the recommendations and past results. Reputation scores based on expertise are used to weight recommendations.

Main Contributions:
- A domain-independent agent-based approach to transition planning that leverages recommendations between agents.
- An extensible framework and set of simple merging algorithms that rely on past execution results rather than complex plan analysis.  
- Demonstrates through simulations that improvements are more due to cooperation between agents rather than the specific plan merging algorithm.
- Provides a useful tool to assist people with cognitive disabilities in transition planning in an autonomous way.
- Opens up new research directions at intersection of planning and recommender systems.

In summary, the paper presents an innovative recommender system-inspired approach for multi-agent based transition planning that is domain-independent and extensible, with usefulness for assisting people with cognitive disabilities.


## Summarize the paper in one sentence.

 Here is a one-sentence summary of the paper:

This paper proposes and compares different ways for autonomous agents to merge transition plans for people with cognitive disabilities by combining past execution results through a reputation system instead of using intrinsic plan restrictions.


## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 According to the paper, the main contributions are:

1. Defining and implementing an agent system that allows the comparison of alternative ways to merge plans through the roles of nodes and operators using the BDI paradigm. 

2. Making assumptions that provide the possibility of evaluating the actions to be included in the plan in a way that does not depend on intrinsic domain-dependent restrictions over the actions.

3. Justifying the use of recommendations and defining a way to weight them according to the past execution of plans, and proposing several (simple) ways to merge them.

4. Testing the performance of the defined merging algorithms with specific setups and observing that the contribution from using recommendations is much greater than from the way plans are merged.

In summary, the main contribution is an innovative agent-based framework for merging plans using recommendations that is not restricted to domain-dependent logic and provides new research directions in the intersection of planning and recommender systems. The system could also help people with cognitive disabilities manage transition planning in an autonomous way.
