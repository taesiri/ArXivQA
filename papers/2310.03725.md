- Stochastic interpolants - The paper introduces a framework of stochastic interpolants to construct continuous-time generative models that map between a simple base density and the target density.

- Data-dependent couplings - The paper proposes constructing dependent couplings between the base and target densities, adapting the base density to the target. This is in contrast to typical independent couplings.

- Conditional generative modeling - The framework allows incorporating conditional information in addition to the dependent coupling. This enables conditional generation adapted to factors like class labels or continuous embeddings. 

- Transport equations - The time-dependent density resulting from the stochastic interpolant process is characterized by a continuity equation. The velocity field solving this transport equation can be learned via regression.

- Image generation - The utility of data-dependent couplings and conditioning is demonstrated on image applications like super-resolution and inpainting. The framework is applied directly to model images in pixel space.

- Base density adaptation - A key contribution is moving beyond standard independent Gaussian base densities to allow the flexibility of data-driven base densities correlated to the target density and task.

So in summary, the key terms cover the stochastic interpolant framework, the use of dependent couplings between base and target densities, incorporation of conditioning variables, the resulting transport characterization, and application to high-dimensional image modeling problems.
