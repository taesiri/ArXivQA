# [Public-private funding models in open source software development: A   case study on scikit-learn](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.06484)

## Write a very high-quality and detailed summary of the paper that describes the paper in a way that a human can fully understand. The summary should cover the problem, the proposed solution and highlight the main contributions of the paper.

 Here is a detailed summary of the key points from the paper:

Problem: 
Governments are increasingly allocating funding for open source software (OSS) development, but it remains an open question how OSS developers view the relative benefits and drawbacks of governmental funding compared to other funding sources. 

Case Study and Research Questions:
This paper explores this question through a case study on scikit-learn, a popular Python machine learning library. The study focuses on three research questions: (1) What are the interests of scikit-learn's public and private funders, and how have they aligned or conflicted with the interests of the maintainers? (2) How do the maintainers view the relative merits and drawbacks of public and private funding? (3) What practical lessons can be drawn about funding models?

Proposed Solution:  
Through 25 interviews with scikit-learn's maintainers and funders, the study illustrates how the maintainers have woven together public and private funding sources into a diversified funding model that ensures the project's sustainability while preserving its community ethos. 

Key Findings:
- Maintainers are motivated by intrinsic incentives and stewarding the community, while companies pursue business interests and governments pursue policy goals. These interests are aligned regarding sustaining scikit-learn.  
- Inria Foundation and governance protocols help maintainers balance varied interests of funders and defend the project's commons-based resources from unwanted influence.
- Diversified funding brings financial stability, acts as a safeguard when disagreements occur, and protects against over-reliance on a single funder.

Contributions:
The study contributes novel findings about the design and role of public-private funding models in OSS. It also provides recommendations for OSS developers on utilizing diversified funding, reminds companies of the value of sponsoring OSS, and advises governments to fund OSS maintenance and engage in dialogue with communities.

## What is the main contribution of this paper?

 According to the paper, the main contributions are:

1. The study illustrates how the maintainers of the scikit-learn project have combined public and private funding sources to ensure the project's continued provision as a digital public good, while using governance protocols to safeguard the community ethos of the project. 

2. The study provides practical recommendations for open source software (OSS) practitioners:

- For OSS developer communities, it shows that diversifying funding sources can help sustain community-led projects while preserving their independence and community values. 

- For companies, it reminds them that sponsoring developers or projects can significantly help overstretched OSS maintainers. 

- For governments, it underscores the importance of funding the maintenance of existing OSS rather than only funding new innovations or features.

In summary, the main contributions are novel findings on emerging public-private funding models in OSS projects, as well as practical insights for OSS stakeholders on sustaining community-led projects through diversified funding.
